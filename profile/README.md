## 🔍 Overview

<a href="#boom3D">Boom 3D</a> enhances Mac audio system-wide — applying equalization, 3D spatial audio, and volume amplification to every audio source on the Mac simultaneously through a system audio driver that intercepts and processes all audio output before it reaches the speakers or headphones.

The <a href="#boom3D">3D surround sound effect</a> creates a spatial audio experience from stereo content — expanding the perceived audio field beyond the physical speaker or headphone positions to create the sensation of audio coming from a wider, more dimensional space. This spatial processing works on any stereo audio source: music, movies, games, video calls, podcasts — any audio playing on the Mac benefits from the surround expansion without the audio source needing to contain surround encoding. The <a href="#boom3D">31-band equalizer</a> provides precise frequency control across the complete audio spectrum — adjusting any combination of frequency bands to compensate for headphone or speaker frequency response characteristics, correct room acoustic issues, or shape the sound signature to personal preference. Named presets for different music genres, content types, and listening contexts provide starting points for equalization without requiring manual band adjustment from scratch.

The <a href="#boom3D">volume booster capability</a> amplifies the Mac's audio output beyond the system's standard maximum volume limit — providing additional loudness when the Mac's maximum volume is insufficient for the listening environment, headphone sensitivity, or audio source level. The boost applies through the software audio driver without distortion within the driver's headroom. The <a href="#boom3D">per-application volume control</a> sets independent volume levels for different running applications — having music playing at one level while notification sounds play at a different level, and video calls at yet another, without adjusting the system master volume.

<a href="#boom3D">Audio effects library</a> provides additional processing options beyond equalization — effects including ambiance, pitch correction, bass boost, fidelity enhancement, and others that shape the audio character beyond frequency adjustment alone. <a href="#boom3D">Headphone and speaker optimization profiles</a> configure Boom 3D's processing for the specific audio output device in use — different optimization settings for headphones versus built-in Mac speakers versus external speakers for appropriate processing in each listening context.

<a href="#boom3D">System-wide processing architecture</a> processes every audio source on the Mac simultaneously — no per-application setup, no application-specific configuration. Any audio playing on the Mac passes through Boom 3D's processing chain regardless of source application. <a href="#boom3D">Menu bar and mini player</a> provides quick access to volume, EQ presets, and the 3D effect toggle from the menu bar without opening the full application.
